# Cooling Credit — Draft International Standard

> **Note:** This document is a proposed draft standard, not an officially adopted standard. It is proposed by Master / inchacomusho / InchaComisho as a conceptual and structural framework.

---

## 1. Purpose

To define Cooling Credit as a distinct credit category that measures, reports, and verifies physical cooling outcomes or the restoration of natural cooling functions — separate from carbon credits, solar geoengineering credits, and adaptation credits.

---

## 2. Definition

A Cooling Credit is a measurable, verifiable, and attributable unit representing one or more of the following outcomes:

1. Direct reduction of ambient air temperature
2. Direct reduction of land surface temperature
3. Direct reduction of water or sea surface temperature
4. Reduction of existing heat load in a defined area or system
5. Restoration or support of water phase transitions (evaporation, condensation, cloud formation, precipitation support, freezing, melting, latent heat transport)
6. Restoration of soil moisture, vegetation transpiration, or hydrological buffering functions
7. Restoration or support of ocean-atmosphere heat exchange and circulation
8. Reduction of climate-related risk pressure through measurable cooling or cooling function restoration

---

## 3. Scope

This standard applies to projects, activities, and interventions that claim to generate Cooling Credits. It defines eligibility criteria, boundary rules, MRV requirements, and exclusion categories.

---

## 4. Distinction from Other Credits

| Credit Type | Primary Mechanism | Is it a Cooling Credit? |
|---|---|---|
| Cooling Credit | Direct temperature reduction or natural cooling function restoration | Yes |
| Carbon Credit | CO2 reduction, removal, or offset | No, unless separately verified direct cooling outcomes exist |
| Sunshade Credit | Blocking or reducing incoming solar radiation | No |
| Reflect Credit | Increasing surface reflectivity or albedo | No, unless separately verified direct cooling outcomes exist |
| Adaptation Credit | Reducing vulnerability or exposure | No, unless physical cooling is verified |
| Resilience Credit | Improving disaster preparedness | No, unless physical cooling is verified |

---

## 5. Draft Status Notice

This document is a proposed framework. It is not an officially adopted international standard. The terminology, classification, and MRV requirements described here are proposed by the author as a contribution to the emerging field of direct planetary cooling governance.
