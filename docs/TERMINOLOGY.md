# Terminology — Cooling Credit Definition

---

## Cooling Credit

A measurable, verifiable, and attributable unit representing direct physical cooling or the restoration of natural cooling functions within Earth's system. It is distinct from carbon credits, sunshade credits, reflect credits, adaptation credits, and resilience credits.

---

## Direct Cooling

A reduction in ambient air temperature, land surface temperature, or water temperature that is directly attributable to an intervention, as measured and verified through MRV.

---

## Existing Heat Load

The accumulated thermal energy stored in a defined area or system in soil, water bodies, urban structures, or air masses that represents an ongoing warming pressure beyond what would exist without prior disturbance of natural cooling functions.

---

## Natural Cooling Function

Any process by which Earth's natural systems transfer, dissipate, convert, or temporarily store heat including evaporation, transpiration, condensation, cloud formation, precipitation, latent heat transport, ocean circulation, and carbon fixation support.

---

## Water-Cycle Cooling

The cooling effect produced by the movement of water through its phase transitions: evaporation (absorbing latent heat), condensation (releasing latent heat aloft), precipitation, and soil moisture retention. Disruption of the water cycle reduces this natural cooling capacity.

---

## Latent Heat Transport

The movement of thermal energy stored in water vapor from warm surface regions to cooler atmospheric layers, where it is released during condensation. Latent heat transport is a primary mechanism by which Earth's surface sheds heat.

---

## Evapotranspiration

The combined process of water evaporation from soil and transpiration from vegetation. Evapotranspiration is a primary natural cooling mechanism for land surfaces. Its restoration is a key eligible activity for Cooling Credits.

---

## Soil Moisture Restoration

The recovery of soil water content to levels that support evapotranspiration, thermal insulation of the surface, and biological activity. Degraded soil moisture is a major contributor to surface overheating.

---

## Ocean-Atmosphere Heat Exchange

The transfer of thermal energy between the ocean surface and the overlying atmosphere, including processes such as evaporation, sensible heat flux, and wind-driven mixing. Ocean-atmosphere heat exchange is a primary mechanism for planetary heat redistribution.

---

## MRV (Measurement, Reporting, and Verification)

The process by which cooling outcomes are quantified (measurement), documented (reporting), and independently confirmed (verification). MRV is a prerequisite for issuing Cooling Credits.
