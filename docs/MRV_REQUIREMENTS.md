# MRV Requirements for Cooling Credits

MRV = Measurement, Reporting, and Verification

---

## 1. Baseline Conditions

Each Cooling Credit issuance unit must document:

- The pre-intervention thermal state of the target area or system
- Ambient air temperature, land surface temperature, water temperature, or WBGT at baseline
- Soil moisture, vegetation cover, or hydrological state at baseline
- The counterfactual: what would have happened without the intervention

---

## 2. Intervention Boundaries

The issuance unit must define:

- Geographic or system boundaries of the intervention
- Duration of monitoring period
- Scope of the cooling outcome being measured

---

## 3. Required Indicators (select applicable)

- Ambient air temperature
- Land surface temperature
- Water or sea surface temperature
- Wet-bulb globe temperature (WBGT)
- Soil moisture
- Evapotranspiration
- Humidity
- Heat flux
- Water retention
- Vegetation recovery indices
- Ocean mixing indicators
- Reduction in energy demand resulting from verified cooling
- Disaster risk pressure reduction linked to verified cooling

---

## 4. Attribution Method

The issuance unit must specify how the cooling outcome is attributed to the intervention, distinguishing from background climate variability, natural fluctuations, and other simultaneous interventions.

---

## 5. Uncertainty Range

The issuance unit must declare the uncertainty range of the cooling outcome measurement.

---

## 6. Additionality

The intervention must demonstrate that the cooling outcome would not have occurred without the project activity.

---

## 7. Permanence and Validity Duration

The issuance unit must specify:

- How long the cooling effect persists
- Whether the credit is time-limited or permanent
- Conditions under which the credit may be reversed or invalidated

---

## 8. Leakage and Displacement Risk

The issuance unit must assess whether the intervention displaces heat load or thermal stress to another area rather than genuinely reducing it.

---

## 9. Third-Party Verification

Cooling Credits must be verified by an independent third party capable of assessing physical cooling outcomes — not only accounting-based outcomes.
