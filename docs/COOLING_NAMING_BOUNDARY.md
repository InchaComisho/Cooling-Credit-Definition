# Cooling Naming Boundary

## Cooling, Shading, Reflection, and Heat-Input Suppression Must Be Separated

This document defines a strict naming boundary for the term **Cooling Credit**.

The word **cooling** should be reserved for actions that directly reduce existing heat load, restore natural cooling functions, or restart / assist physical natural processes that move, release, circulate, or dissipate heat.

It should not be used for actions that merely block, shade, reflect, or delay incoming solar radiation without reducing the heat already accumulated in the Earth system.

---

## 1. Core Definition

Only the following should be called **cooling** in the context of Cooling Credits:

```text
Actions that reduce existing heat load on Earth,
or actions that restart, restore, or complement natural cooling phenomena,
especially water-cycle and phase-transition processes.
```

This includes actions that produce or restore:

- direct heat-load reduction;
- air, surface, soil, water, or sea-surface temperature reduction;
- evapotranspiration;
- evaporation-based latent heat transfer;
- soil moisture recovery;
- water retention and circulation;
- cloud / rain / hydrological recovery caused by restored natural processes;
- ocean mixing, oxygenation, and heat-exchange recovery;
- waste-heat reduction;
- natural cooling-function restoration in forests, soils, wetlands, cities, and oceans.

---

## 2. What Should Not Be Called Cooling

The following should not be classified as **Cooling Credits** by default:

- solar shielding;
- space sunshades;
- stratospheric aerosol injection;
- artificial sunlight blocking;
- mirror-based reflection systems;
- albedo-only measures;
- surface whitening that only reflects incoming sunlight;
- shade-only systems without verified heat-load reduction;
- any project that reduces incoming solar radiation but does not reduce existing heat load or restore natural cooling functions.

These may be discussed under other categories, such as:

- Sunshade Credit;
- Shade Credit;
- Reflect Credit;
- Albedo Credit;
- Heat-Input Suppression Credit;
- Solar-Radiation-Management Credit.

They should not automatically be called Cooling Credits.

---

## 3. Why the Distinction Matters

Blocking sunlight is not the same as cooling the Earth.

A sunshade may reduce part of future incoming radiation.  
A reflective surface may redirect part of sunlight.  
An aerosol layer may modify radiative balance.

However, these actions do not automatically:

- remove existing heat from oceans;
- reduce stored urban heat;
- restore soil moisture;
- restart evapotranspiration;
- restore forest cooling functions;
- improve water-cycle circulation;
- recover ocean oxygenation;
- reduce artificial waste heat;
- lower actual heat stress in communities;
- produce locally measurable cooling outcomes.

Therefore, they must not be equated with Cooling Credits unless they independently pass the Cooling Credit definition, boundary rule, and MRV requirements.

---

## 4. The Water Phase-Transition Principle

Natural cooling on Earth is strongly connected to water and phase transitions.

Important natural cooling processes include:

- evaporation;
- transpiration;
- evapotranspiration;
- condensation;
- cloud formation;
- rainfall;
- soil moisture retention;
- groundwater recharge;
- ocean-atmosphere heat exchange;
- latent heat transport.

Cooling Credit eligibility should prioritize actions that restore or complement these processes.

Examples include:

- restoring soil moisture so evaporation and plant transpiration can resume;
- restoring forests and mixed vegetation so evapotranspiration can recover;
- restoring wetlands, rivers, and urban waterways;
- using rainwater, reclaimed water, or circulated water for evaporative cooling;
- reducing waste heat from buildings, infrastructure, and industry;
- supporting ocean circulation and oxygenation under strict ecological monitoring.

---

## 5. Naming Rule

The naming rule is simple:

```text
If it cools by reducing existing heat load or restoring natural cooling processes,
it may be evaluated as a Cooling Credit.

If it only blocks sunlight, creates shade, or reflects radiation,
it should be named as shading, sunshade, reflection, albedo, or heat-input suppression.
```

The term **Cooling Credit** should not be used as a broad marketing label for every project that claims to slow warming.

---

## 6. Market Integrity Rule

A project should not sell itself as a Cooling Credit merely because it uses the words “cooling,” “climate cooling,” or “planetary cooling.”

To qualify, it must demonstrate:

1. verified cooling or natural cooling-function restoration;
2. a clear baseline;
3. measurable indicators;
4. MRV;
5. risk disclosure;
6. duration and validity period;
7. distinction from carbon offsetting, solar shielding, and reflectivity-only claims.

Without these, the project should not be issued as a Cooling Credit.

---

## 7. Summary

Cooling Credits should be limited to actions that:

```text
reduce existing Earth-system heat load,
or restore / restart natural cooling phenomena,
especially water-cycle and phase-transition processes.
```

Solar shielding, space sunshades, mirrors, albedo-only measures, and stratospheric aerosol approaches may affect incoming radiation, but they do not by themselves constitute Cooling Credits.

They should be classified with accurate terms such as **Sunshade**, **Shade**, **Reflect**, **Albedo**, or **Heat-Input Suppression**, not **Cooling**, unless verified physical cooling outcomes and natural cooling-function recovery are demonstrated under MRV.
