# Excluded Categories — What is NOT a Cooling Credit

The following credit categories must not be labelled as Cooling Credits unless they separately produce verified direct cooling outcomes or verified restoration of natural cooling functions that meet Cooling Credit MRV requirements.

For the strict naming distinction between **cooling**, **sunshade**, **shade**, **reflect**, **albedo**, and **heat-input suppression**, see [Cooling Naming Boundary](COOLING_NAMING_BOUNDARY.md).

---

## Core Exclusion Principle

A project is not a Cooling Credit merely because it claims to slow warming, reduce sunlight, reflect radiation, improve climate resilience, or reduce emissions.

To qualify as a Cooling Credit, it must demonstrate one or more of the following:

- reduction of existing heat load;
- measurable temperature reduction;
- restoration of water-cycle cooling;
- restoration of water phase transitions;
- restoration of latent heat transport;
- restoration of soil moisture and evapotranspiration;
- restoration of ocean-atmosphere heat exchange;
- restoration of natural cooling functions.

If it only reduces incoming solar radiation, creates shade, increases reflectivity, changes carbon accounting, or reduces vulnerability without physical cooling, it belongs in another category.

---

## Carbon Credits

Carbon Credits reduce, remove, or offset greenhouse gas emissions. They do not, by default, demonstrate:

- Direct temperature reduction
- Reduction of existing heat load
- Water-cycle cooling recovery
- Soil moisture recovery
- Evapotranspiration recovery
- Ocean-atmosphere circulation recovery
- Measurable reduction in heat stress

Carbon Credits and Cooling Credits are separate categories.

---

## Sunshade Credits

Interventions whose primary goal is to block sunlight, shade land surfaces, or reduce incoming solar radiation should be classified as Sunshade Credits, not Cooling Credits.

Examples:

- Stratospheric aerosol injection
- Orbital sunshades
- Space-based solar shields
- Large-scale atmospheric solar blocking systems
- Geoengineering methods primarily aimed at reducing incoming solar radiation

These methods may reduce incoming solar energy, but they do not necessarily restore water phase transitions, soil moisture, transpiration, ocean circulation, or dissipate existing heat load.

Reducing incoming sunlight is **heat-input suppression**, not automatically cooling.

---

## Reflect Credits

Interventions whose primary goal is to increase surface reflectivity or albedo should be classified as Reflect Credits, not Cooling Credits.

Examples:

- Ground-based reflectors
- White-painted surfaces
- Reflective roofs
- Reflective pavements
- Desert reflective panels
- Mirror-based surface reflectivity systems

These methods may reflect sunlight or reduce local heat absorption, but their primary mechanism is reflection, not direct cooling or natural cooling function restoration.

Reflection should be named as reflection or albedo increase unless verified physical cooling outcomes are separately demonstrated.

---

## Adaptation Credits

Interventions that reduce vulnerability or exposure to climate impacts without producing measurable cooling outcomes should be classified as Adaptation Credits.

---

## Resilience Credits

Interventions that improve disaster preparedness without producing measurable cooling outcomes should be classified as Resilience Credits.

---

## Solar Geoengineering Credits

Any credit arising from solar geoengineering interventions, including stratospheric aerosol injection, marine cloud brightening aimed solely at reflectivity, space reflectors, or orbital sunshades, should not be classified as Cooling Credits unless separately verified direct cooling outcomes or natural cooling-function restoration are documented.

Solar geoengineering may alter the radiation balance, but it does not automatically remove existing Earth-system heat load, restore water cycles, restore soil moisture, restart evapotranspiration, restore ocean oxygenation, or reduce local heat stress under MRV.

---

## Summary

The naming rule is:

```text
If it reduces existing heat load or restores natural cooling processes, it may be evaluated as Cooling Credit.
If it only blocks, shades, reflects, or changes accounting, it must be classified elsewhere.
```
